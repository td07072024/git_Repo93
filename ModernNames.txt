Current names of the sea are usually equivalents of the English name "Black Sea", including these given in the countries bordering the sea:[9]

Abkhaz: Амшын Еиқәа, romanized: Amŝən Ejkʷa, IPA: [ɑmʂɨn ɛjkʷʰɑ]
Adyghe: Хы Шӏуцӏэ, romanized: Xə Šʷʼucʼɛ, IPA: [xɘ ʃʷʼtsʼɜ]
Armenian: Սեւ ծով, romanized: Sev cov, IPA: [sɛv t͡sɔv]
Azerbaijani: Qara dəniz, IPA: [ɡɑˈɾɑ dæniz]
Bulgarian: Чeрно морe, romanized: Čérno moré, IPA: [ˈt͡ʃɛrno moˈrɛ]
Crimean Tatar: Qara deñiz, Къара денъиз, IPA: [qɑrɑ deŋiz]
Georgian: შავი ზღვა, romanized: shavi zghva, IPA: [ʃavi zʁʷa]
Laz and Mingrelian: უჩა ზუღა, romanized: Ucha Zugha, IPA: [ˈutʃä ˈzuɣä], or simply ზუღა, Zugha, IPA: [ˈzuɣä], "Sea"
Romanian: Marea Neagră, pronounced [ˈmare̯a ˈne̯aɡrə] ⓘ
Russian: Чёрное мо́ре, romanized: Čórnoje móre, IPA: [ˈt͡ɕɵrnəjə ˈmorʲe]
Turkish: Karadeniz, IPA: [kaˈɾadeniz]
Ukrainian: Чо́рне мо́ре, romanized: Čórne móre, IPA: [ˈt͡ʃɔrne ˈmɔre]
Such names have not yet been shown conclusively to predate the 13th century.[10]

In Greece, the historical name "Euxine Sea", which holds a different literal meaning (see below), is still widely used:

Greek: Εύξεινος Πόντος, romanized: Éfxinos Póndos, lit. 'Hospitable Sea', [ˈefksinos ˈpondos]; the name Μαύρη Θάλασσα, Mávri Thálassa, 'Black Sea', [ˈmavɾi ˈθalasa], is used, but is much less common.[citation needed]
The Black Sea is one of four seas named in English after common color terms – the others being the Red Sea, the White Sea and the Yellow Sea.
